Identity

Ranade · #E8B84A

Concise is a quiet, near-black workbench for the student who already knows what they want to learn. The interface stays out of the way so source material — equations, diagrams, a student's own writing — is what reads loudest. The brand mark concentrates that idea into a single typographic gesture.

2.4 The accent — one warm note
--accent · #E8B84A

Reserved for two places only.

The accent is the wordmark's dot and the avatar's ring — nothing else. It is a quiet signature, not a paint bucket. Status colour, buttons, links, and highlights all stay neutral. If a future surface earns the accent, we add it deliberately, one site at a time.

Iconography

Heroicons · monochrome only

Heroicons is the entire system — mini inside inputs, outline on sidebar tabs, solid on the settings cog, micro for file-type badges. Stroke is 1.5px throughout. No icon ever carries colour beyond the foreground scale. No emoji, no unicode glyphs.

Motion

Quiet · purposeful · no bounce

Motion in Concise is a sidekick, not the show. Hovers swap an alpha tier, presses flip the inner highlight to an inner shadow, popovers fade-and-rise by 4 pixels, dialogs by 8. Nothing overshoots. Nothing scales. The student stays oriented.

Hover
Background steps up one alpha tier — e.g. white/046 → white/096. No scale, no translate.
90 msease-out
Press
Inner top-highlight flips to inset 0 1px 2px rgba(0,0,0,0.20). The button pushes in.

Fade in. 4px translateY from below.

Fade in. 8px translateY from below. Scrim fades over 200ms.

08

Voice

Utilitarian · second-person · no hedging

Concise talks to the student, not about itself. Section titles are nouns. Prompts pose a single question. Dialog titles are imperative verbs. Math vocabulary appears uncut — the audience knows what these words mean. We don't apologise for the domain.

Do
"What do you want to learn today?"
Open with a single question. Second person, no preamble. The user is here to do work — say so.
Don't
"Welcome back! Let's get started!"
No exclamation marks. No marketing register. The app is a workbench, not a landing page.
Do
"Build Concept" · "Add Fragments" · "Concept Store"
Imperative verbs for actions. Title Case nouns for sections. Short, specific, lifted from the domain.
Don't
"Generate with AI" · "Smart Suggestions"
Never reference the model by name. Actions read as if the student performed them.
Do
Search...
Placeholders are sentence case with a lowercase ellipsis (three dots, not Unicode "…").
Don't
"Type to search your stuff…"
No filler. No "your stuff". Specific or omitted.
